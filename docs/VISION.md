# Delivery Intelligence Platform

> **Transform engineering delivery data into actionable intelligence.**
>
> A Delivery Intelligence Platform that continuously observes software delivery, measures flow efficiency, forecasts outcomes, and acts as an AI-powered Agile Coach and Engineering Advisor.

---

# Vision

Software teams generate enormous amounts of delivery data every day, but very little actionable intelligence.

Most engineering tools answer questions like:

- What issues are open?
- Who is assigned to this task?
- Which sprint are we in?

Very few answer the questions Engineering Managers actually care about:

- Why is delivery slowing down?
- Where is the current bottleneck?
- Which project is at risk?
- Which team needs attention today?
- What should I improve next?
- How is engineering performance impacting business outcomes?
- Are our process improvements actually working?

The goal of this project is to bridge that gap.

The platform continuously collects delivery events from engineering tools, transforms them into a unified delivery model, calculates Flow Metrics inspired by Lean and Kanban, connects those metrics to business outcomes, and uses an LLM to provide continuous coaching and decision support.

Rather than becoming another project management tool, the platform becomes an **Engineering Intelligence System**.

---

# Product Principles

## Delivery Intelligence, not Project Management

The platform does not replace tools like Linear or Jira.

Instead, it observes them.

It is a layer of intelligence that sits above delivery systems.

---

## AI Explains, Statistics Predict

Forecasts should never rely solely on LLMs.

Statistical models produce predictions.

The LLM explains those predictions.

Examples:

- Monte Carlo Forecasting
- Lead Time Distributions
- Little's Law
- Percentile Analysis
- Flow Metrics
- Risk Scoring

---

## Events are the Source of Truth

The system stores immutable delivery events rather than mutable issue state.

Everything else is derived.

Examples:

- Issue Created
- Assigned
- Started
- Blocked
- Unblocked
- Completed
- Pull Request Opened
- Pull Request Merged
- Deployment
- Incident

This enables historical analysis and reproducible metrics.

---

## Platform Agnostic

The domain model should never depend on Linear-specific concepts.

Instead of storing "Linear Issues", store "Work Items".

Connectors translate external systems into a common domain.

---

## Explainability First

Every recommendation generated by AI should be explainable.

The platform should always answer:

- Why was this recommendation generated?
- Which metrics support it?
- Which best practices were used?
- Which historical evidence supports it?

---

## Continuous Improvement

The objective is not reporting.

The objective is helping Engineering Managers continuously improve delivery performance.

# Architectural Layers

## Layer 1 — Connectors

Responsible for collecting delivery events.

Responsibilities:

- Synchronize external systems
- Normalize data
- Generate immutable events
- Preserve timestamps
- Avoid business logic

---

## Layer 2 — Delivery Intelligence

The heart of the platform.

Responsibilities:

- Calculate Flow Metrics
- Detect bottlenecks
- Generate forecasts
- Produce risk indicators
- Build historical snapshots
- Correlate engineering metrics with business outcomes

---

## Layer 3 — AI Intelligence

Responsible for reasoning.

The AI never calculates metrics.

Instead, it:

- explains metrics
- prioritizes problems
- suggests improvements
- identifies patterns
- coaches Engineering Managers
